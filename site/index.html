Every check Penny runs is loaded with what a great controller already knows: your actual policy thresholds (from your database, not hardcoded), what normal looks like for that store and that season, a library of known-innocent edge cases to rule out first, and exactly which human each finding should reach. A three-person army

Meet Builder Tribe

It started with a shrug. One of us watched a store manager wave off a drawer that came up twelve dollars short — again — because chasing it would cost more than the twelve dollars. That shrug wouldn't leave us alone. We dug in, and the deeper we went, the worse it got: the same quiet leaks in procurement, cash handling, and margin at every multi-unit operator we met. Conversations with operators from chains like Chick-fil-A and Jollibee turned a hunch into conviction — this is a real, expensive, unsolved problem, and everyone had simply learned to live with it. So the three of us stopped talking about it and built Penny.

And yes — she's named after the neighbor from The Big Bang Theory. Except our Penny is her financial opposite: she watches every cent, rules out the innocent explanations first, and never guesses.
