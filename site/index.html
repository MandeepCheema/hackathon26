The controls gap

Leaks hide in the noise. Old tools make it worse.

Money leaves through a thousand small doors — a till that's quietly short, an invoice billed twice, a cashier voiding sales. The tools meant to catch it either miss everything or flag everything.

🚨

Rules engines cry wolf

A fixed threshold fires on all 365 anomalies — 269 rounding blips, 55 legit recurring orders. Teams drown in false alarms and stop looking.

🧍

Analysts can't scale

A human can reconcile a handful of stores. Not 2,000, every night, across cash, cards, invoices, and shifts. The long tail never gets read.

🎭

The real leaks are disguised

The obvious signal is usually the decoy. Distinguishing a skim from a counting error, a duplicate from a weekly order, takes judgment — not a query.
